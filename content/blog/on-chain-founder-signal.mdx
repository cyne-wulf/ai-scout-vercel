Founder signal gets weaker when it is filtered through decks, social posts, and carefully rehearsed claims. AI Scout starts from a different source: live founder behavior in a real Silicon Valley pitch room.

The useful signal is not only what a founder says. It is also how they answer follow-ups, handle pressure, explain the demo, respond to investor skepticism, and connect the product to a market.

When that interaction is captured, structured, and anchored, it becomes a more durable primitive for Web3 fundraising:

- Investors get context that is closer to the room.
- DAOs can review evidence instead of relying on summaries alone.
- AI agents can reason over transcripts, sentiment, and follow-up trails.
- Founders get a public record of what they actually presented.

This is the core idea behind AI Scout: make the live pitch event machine-readable, auditable, and useful for capital allocation.

The goal is not to replace human judgment. The goal is to give human investors, DAOs, and agentic workflows a better substrate for judgment.
