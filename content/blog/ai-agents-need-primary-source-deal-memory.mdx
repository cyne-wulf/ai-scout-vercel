AI agents are getting better at reading decks, summarizing websites, and comparing startups. That creates leverage, but it also creates a problem: polished material is increasingly easy to generate.

If every founder can produce a beautiful deck, a confident script, and a clean market narrative, investors and agents need a source of truth that sits closer to reality.

Primary-source deal memory is one answer.

For AI Scout, that means capturing the live pitch, demo, Q&A, investor reactions, and follow-up signals as structured records. The agent does not only read the founder's polished narrative. It can inspect what happened in the room.

That changes the quality of downstream workflows:

- Summaries can cite actual pitch moments.
- Comparisons can include live Q&A strength.
- Risk review can flag unsupported claims.
- DAO voters can inspect the same evidence.
- Future updates can be compared against the original record.

The agentic AI era rewards clean inputs. AI Scout is designed around that idea: better primary-source deal memory makes better autonomous investment workflows possible.
